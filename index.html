Independent Study: Maximum Particle Velocity in Acoustofluidics

Role: Researcher Date: January – June 2015 Organization: UCSD (Professor James Friend)

Problem Statement

I joined Professor Friend’s lab in 2017 after taking his class on advanced analytical methods for solving non-linear ODEs. Professor Friend noticed a trend across his high-frequency vibration experiments: the maximum particle velocity in a solid vibration wave never exceeded 1 meter per second, regardless of frequency. We believed this to be a fundamental property of nature.

My Role

Professor Friend and I dug into the fundamental equations and discovered a theoretical velocity limit for each material where it would experience cyclical yield stress and fail. We called this "vmax".

I used MATLAB to simulate tens of thousands of random material samples for 11 different materials (metals, plastics, ceramics, etc.) using Monte Carlo analysis. We introduced imperfection factors (Ψ) to model real-world flaws.

We found that the likelihood of failure around the 1 m/s threshold was consistently high (around 50% or greater) across all materials.

Project Outcome

My Monte Carlo model became the basis for a paper published in the Journal of the Acoustical Society of America (JASA). After I graduated, the work was continued by Naiqing Zhang, and the project was a resounding success.

ViOptix Intra.Ox Disposable O-Ring Design

Role: Mechanical Engineering Intern Date: June – August 2018 Company: Triple Ring Technologies / ViOptix

Problem Statement

Triple Ring was developing a handheld tissue oximeter called the "Intra.Ox" for ViOptix. The device helps surgeons determine if tissue is oxygenated enough to survive post-surgery.

The system consisted of a reusable "durable" unit and disposable components (battery pack and sheath). The sheath needed to protect the durable from contamination in the operating room.

My Role

I was responsible for designing the custom O-ring for the disposable sheath. The challenge was to create a seal that was soft enough to be closed by a nurse with less than 4 lbs of force, but reliable enough to prevent leaks under low pressure differentials.

We modeled the seal design off a Pelican™ Case to create local compression points. I determined that a 15 Shore A hardness was optimal. We worked with RD Rubber to develop a manufacturing process that ensured the custom O-rings met our strict stiffness requirements (13-17 Shore A).

Project Outcome

The design passed FDA clearance. ViOptix now manufactures approximately 2,000 disposable sheaths annually, and the device is used in hospitals across the country.

Auris Health Viewer Console

Role: Mechanical Engineer Date: August 2019 – August 2020 Company: Triple Ring Technologies (Client: Auris Health)

Problem Statement

Auris Health hired Triple Ring to help design and manufacture the viewer console for their robotic surgical system, intended to compete with the Da Vinci robot. The goal was to create a lighter, smaller, and sleeker console.

My Role

This was my first full-time project. I managed the Bill of Materials (BOM) and coordinated with vendors for complex parts, including the optical housings which required precise machining and anodizing.

I co-wrote the Manufacturing Process Instructions (MPI) using CAD exploded views and later, real-life photos from the first builds.

We built multiple iterations (Alpha, Beta) and 7 Design Verification Testing (DVT) units. We utilized Vantablack coating from Surrey NanoSystems to minimize light reflection within the console.

Project Outcome

We successfully delivered 7 DVT units that met optical specifications. However, following Auris Health's acquisition by Johnson & Johnson, the project faced integration challenges and was eventually deprioritized by the new parent company.

BOM and Inventory Tracking Process Development

Role: Mechanical Engineer Date: June 2022 – December 2023 Company: Triple Ring Technologies

Problem Statement

Use SolidWorks to automate the creation of Bill of Materials (BOM) and inventory tracking, replacing a prone-to-error manual Excel process.

My Role

I integrated our PDM software (Windchill) with SolidWorks to manage metadata like vendor names and costs. I interpreted and wrote scripts to parse SolidWorks assembly data into text files, which could then be imported into formatted Excel templates.

This allowed for automatic generation of indented BOMs and cost calculations (COGS).

I also developed an inventory tracking template that compares existing inventory against new BOM requirements, flagging changes and calculate surplus/deficits.

Project Outcome

The new process significantly reduced errors and time spent on manual data entry, providing accurate, real-time cost and inventory tracking for engineering teams. It has been adopted company-wide for projects of all sizes.
